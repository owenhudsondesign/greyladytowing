## 4. BRAND IDENTITY ANALYSIS

### Name Origin & Significance
"Grey Lady" is the historic nickname for Nantucket Island itself, derived from the characteristic fog that blankets the island. ## APPENDIX A: BEACH DRIVING GUIDE CONTENT

*Source: greyladytowing.com/how-to-drive-on-beach-1*

### Current Page Headline
"How do you drive on the beach? There are a few things you should know to succeed"

### Section 1: PERMITS/STICKERS
- Beach sticker required before driving
- **Town of Nantucket Beach Sticker** - covers most Nantucket beaches
- **Trustees Great Point Sticker** - required for Great Point and Coatue
- Order before June for discounted pricing
- Gatehouse is ideal location for Great Point permit

### Section 2: VEHICLE CHOICE
**Recommended Vehicles:**
- Pickup trucks
- Jeeps
- Toyota 4Runners
- Ford Broncos
- Similar tall SUVs with genuine 4Ã—4 systems
- AWD can work but may struggle

**NOT Recommended:**
- Electric vehicles
- Crossover SUVs with limited clearance
- Sedans
- Anything with two-wheel drive

### Section 3: TIPS & TRICKS
- Deflate tires to **15 PSI** (12 PSI for extremely soft sand)
- Use **4HI** normally; switch to **4LO** when sand is soft
- Avoid hard braking or acceleration
- "Keep your speed and momentum - this can get you over any obstacle or tricky spot"
- Never drive on dunes
- Pack out all trash

## APPENDIX B: NANTUCKET CONTEXT

### Why "Grey Lady"
Nantucket is nicknamed "The Grey Lady" due to the characteristic fog that often shrouds the island. When fog rolled in thick during early mornings and late evenings, sailors would say they were heading to "The Grey Lady." This name carries deep maritime heritage and local pride.

### Beach Driving on Nantucket
- Requires special permit
- Popular beaches for driving: Surfside, Madaket, Great Point, Coatue
- Sand conditions vary with tides and weather
- Rental vehicles commonly get stuck
- Local expertise is valuable (knowing tide schedules, soft spots)

### Seasonal Considerations
- **Summer:** High tourist traffic, more stuck rental vehicles, busy season
- **Off-season:** Locals, year-round residents, different challenges (weather)
- Website should serve both audiences
